World's All Time Top 3 Books :

"Don Quixote" by Miguel de Cervantes:

Considered one of the greatest works of fiction, "Don Quixote" is a masterpiece of Spanish literature. Published in two parts in 1605 and 1615, it tells the story of an eccentric nobleman who becomes convinced he is a knight-errant and embarks on a series of adventures. It is renowned for its humor, satire, and exploration of idealism and reality.

"Pride and Prejudice" by Jane Austen:

This beloved novel by Jane Austen is a timeless classic of English literature. Set in the early 19th century, it follows the headstrong Elizabeth Bennet as she navigates societal expectations, love, and personal growth. With Austen's wit, sharp social commentary, and memorable characters, "Pride and Prejudice" has become a beloved tale of romance and social dynamics.

"To Kill a Mockingbird" by Harper Lee:

Published in 1960, this powerful American novel addresses themes of racial injustice, compassion, and the loss of innocence. Set in the 1930s in a racially divided Alabama town, it tells the story of Scout Finch, a young girl who witnesses her father's defense of a falsely accused black man. "To Kill a Mockingbird" continues to resonate for its compelling narrative, evocative atmosphere, and exploration of important social issues.
