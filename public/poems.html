World's All Time Top 3 Poems :

"The Divine Comedy" by Dante Alighieri :

This epic poem, written in the 14th century, is considered one of the greatest literary works of all time. Divided into three parts - Inferno, Purgatorio, and Paradiso - it follows Dante's journey through Hell, Purgatory, and Heaven. "The Divine Comedy" explores themes of sin, redemption, and the complexities of human existence, and it showcases Dante's masterful use of allegory, vivid imagery, and profound philosophical insights.

"The Waste Land" by T.S. Eliot :

Published in 1922, this modernist poem is a complex and layered exploration of post-World War I disillusionment and cultural decay. "The Waste Land" reflects the fragmented and disillusioned state of society through its diverse voices, allusions, and haunting imagery. It has had a profound influence on 20th-century poetry, exemplifying the shift in poetic expression during that era.

"Sonnet 18" (commonly known as "Shall I compare thee to a summer's day?") by William Shakespeare :

One of Shakespeare's most beloved sonnets, Sonnet 18 is a testament to the enduring power of poetry and love. It celebrates the beauty and immortalization of a loved one through the art of verse. With its exquisite language, musicality, and timeless themes, Sonnet 18 is widely regarded as one of the greatest sonnets ever written.
